How It's Made

From Earth
To Your Space

Every StoneCore garden goes through four deliberate stages — each one done slowly, carefully, entirely by hand. Here is what happens before your piece reaches you.

The ritual
01
🌿
Clay Selection

We blend raw stoneware and porcelain clays by hand, ensuring just the right plasticity. Each batch is tested for texture, shrinkage, and character — because the foundation decides the soul.

Stoneware Porcelain Blend Hand-Kneaded
02
🏺
Throwing & Carving

Each stone lantern, bridge pillar, and basin is thrown on a kick wheel or carved from solid clay blocks. We use traditional Japanese kanna tools to refine textures that mimic weathered stone.

Kick Wheel Hand Carving Weathered Finish
03
🏮
Handcrafting

This is where the garden comes alive. Each miniature shrine, monk figurine, lantern, and bridge is hand-painted with fine brushes and then placed — by eye, by feel — into the composition. There is no template. Every placement decision is made in the moment by the artisan, which is why no two pieces are ever identical. We work slowly. We don't rush this step.

Hand-Painted Miniature Shrines Monk Figurines No Templates
04
🍃
Glaze & Kiln

We fire each garden twice. The first bisque firing opens the clay's pores; then we apply custom ash glazes and copper accents before the final cone-10 reduction firing. The results are unpredictable — in the best way.

Ash Glaze Reduction Firing Cone 10 Copper Accents
05
✨
Kintsugi Assembly

Instead of hiding imperfections, we celebrate them. Cracks are filled with urushi lacquer dusted with gold powder — the art of golden repair. Each garden becomes more beautiful for its scars.

Urushi Gold Dust Kintsugi Wabi-Sabi
After Delivery

Caring For Your
Zen Garden

Your StoneCore garden is low-maintenance by design. The preserved moss and dried bonsai need no water or sunlight — just keep them away from a few things and they'll last for years.

Shop Now →
💧
No Water Needed Keep away from direct water and excessive humidity. The preserved elements don't need watering.
☀️
Indirect Light Only Place in indirect sunlight. Direct harsh sun may fade the moss colour over time.
🧹
Gentle Dusting Dust gently with a soft dry cloth or a small brush. Do not use wet cloths or sprays.
📍
Stable Surface Place on a flat, stable surface away from high traffic areas. Avoid spots where it may be knocked over.
